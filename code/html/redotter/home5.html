Artist's Statement

But Tonemah and those with him crossed through this time-transormation in one lifetime. When my grandfather, Tonemah (Quote-Ko-Keah), was a young man he and his brother, along with other Kiowas, raided settlements in Texas and Mexico.

One of Tonemah's brothers was killed during one of these raids. My grandfather continued the warlike traditions until missionaries came to Saddle Mountain. Then he heard the good news of the Jesus Way and his life changed.

"What Tribe Are You...German?"

Near Saddle Mountain he built a house. It looked like the other large German houses of the nearby wheat farmers—until you went inside. Then you noticed that the windows were very close to the floors. Although my grandfather had lived only in teepees, he built the house perfectly except for a few details.

I belive that much of this native strength came from a quality characteristic of the warlike Kiowa. Sayn-Day, the hero of their Trickster-Hero stories, survived by cunning subterfuge and adaptability under adversity. Native Intelligence and Kiowa Chutzpah as well as loyalty to friends and family mean that the Kiowa People are survivors.
