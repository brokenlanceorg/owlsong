Artist's Statement

His inheritance is his memory of a former life that will not be in the time to come. His legacy to his descendants is an inherited grief—the irony of an alienated dasein in native soil.

"Broken Lance"

The inheritor described above is based on a real person—Tonemah—born circa 1854 in what is today Southwestern Oklahoma.

"Saddle Mountain"

The inheritor is based on my memory of Tonemah as I knew him as a boy. I remember him sitting quietly watching us play, or speaking in Kiowa and laughing with the relatives, telling stories.

Sometimes he would talk about the old times. Sometimes he would talk about modern events. He belonged to both worlds, to both times. Amazingly, he navigated through both with skill and grace; he was a successful warrior as a young man and a successful farmer in later life. He was the first person—White or Indian—to own a car in his county.

Tonemah and others of his generation found themselves suspended between two worlds—the Old Order of Kiowa traditions and cosmology, and the New Order of western civilization and Christianity.
