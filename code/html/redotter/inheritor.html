"The Inheritor"
20"x16" Acrylic on Canvas
His room gives a window-view of the land that is his inheritance. He remembers his youth when this land was fought for and promised in treaty by the agents from Washington. And he remembers as a boy hearing of when the same land was fought for and held against traditional and ancient enemies - the other tribes. The familiar mountains, and canyons, and rivers - he knows each by its real name and its significance in Kiowa history, stories and fables. He remembers curling up to sleep as a little boy late at night watching the fire while listening to the rise and fall of his grandmother's voice re-telling the old familiar stories at the campsite not far from his window view. The land of his youth is still much the same. But her stories, and faces, lovers, enemies, dreams, and visions of his youth are not found in the present. It's a time of transition - the past lingers but the Modern Age expands inexorably. There are new stories and new people, new kinds of clothes, wooden beds, houses, and sermons in church. He wears his white shirt and sits in a modern chair but keeps his hair braided. And he keeps a bed in every room so everyone has a place to sleep when visiting. The former times are, like him, aged and muted. His inheritance is his memory of a former life that will not be in the time to come. His legacy to his descendants is an inherited grief - the irony of an alienated dasein in native soil.
