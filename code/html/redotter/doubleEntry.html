"Double Entry"
14"x18" Acrylic on Panel
The capital that is a measure of the land's value is tabulated and organized in the ledger. The cash value of the land's production is a new way of ascribing worth to an element that is at once a birthright and priceless. How do you ascribe value to an element as necessary yet as ubiquitous as water, light or air? What made him strong as a Kiowa warrior—discernment, observation and guarded calculation—make him a prosperous farmer. Like the folk-hero Tsayn-Day, he finds himself walking along into problems and coming out of them successfully—adapting and familiarizing himself to new situations. So the chagrin is felt keenly when he finds a double-entry in the farm ledger. This farm is the same land whereupon the Kadl-Hih buffalo grew fat, and where he and his brothers hunted, from buffalo to wheat, from counting coup to counting on the market.
