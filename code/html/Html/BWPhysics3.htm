These possiblities are grand in scope; they can be as simple as proving that it's possible to create fire, or as significant as modifying epistemology, ontology, and metaphysics in one stupefyingly elegant theorem.

For example, Special Relativity demonstrates that time and length dilation/contraction is possible. General Relativity shows us that black holes (regions of space-time that have folded in on themselves) are possible. General Relativity shows us that space-time is warped by the presence of matter, and that wormholes (distorted regions of space-time that connect two regions through higher dimensions) are possible. Simple physics shows that it's possible to write the entire contents of the encyclopedia Britannica on the head of a pin. Quantum Physics has shown that entanglement is real.

Conversely, physics demonstrates certain impossiblities. Thermodynamics shows that the entropy (the measure of disorder or unavailable energy) in any closed system always increases. Thus, it's impossible to create an engine that requires no fuel, or worse still: if the universe doesn't contract upon itself in a 'big crunch', the universe will suffer 'heat death' in which the universe slowly cools and very little energy can be extracted. Quantum Physics shows that uncertainty is built into observing the universe, so that exact knowledge of a particle's position/momentum, time/energy, and other dual qualities of nature is impossible.

Can we imagine a world without physics? I can't even begin to. Even the simple acts of basics motor skills such as walking, talking, and moving require physics calculations in one's mind (even if they are largely unconscious).
