The Baptist faith is not without it’s shameful and fallacious logic.

For example, consider the definition of God presented earlier (and note also the use of the masculine and anthropomorphic possessive pronoun ‘his’): “There is one and only one living and true God. He is an intelligent, spiritual, and personal Being, the Creator, Redeemer, Preserver, and Ruler of the universe. God is infinite in holiness and all other perfections. God is all powerful and all knowing; and His perfect knowledge extends to all things, past, present, and future, including the future decisions of His free creatures.”

The definition states that God is omniscient and his creatures have free will. This is completely impossible. A being having free will means that no other entity knows or causes that being’s actions, thoughts, etc. Free will implies that only I know and control what I’m going to do today, and more importantly that no other being knows or controls what I’m going to do today, thus God is not omniscient, but by the Baptist’s definition God is omniscient-a logical contradiction, therefore an impossibility.

To get around the contradiction temporarily one could continue to assume that God is omniscient and omnipotent, but that he placed barriers that prevent us from becoming omniscient theoretically (i.e., quantum uncertainty ) and thereby create the illusion that we have free will, although everything may still be determined precisely outside the quantum system. In this scenario, there are those of God’s creations that will go to hell, and those that go to heaven.
