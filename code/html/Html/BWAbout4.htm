We had broken the bonds of Plato’s cave and ascended to the mountaintop where the sun shines the brightest and one can see the farthest. In honor of Tolkien, we christened the dirt pile ‘The Shire’ (actually a more appropriate name would have been ‘Hobbiton’, but ‘The Shire’ seemed more euphonic).

[Destruction of Shire]
The rednecks at the chicken factory decided that we were having too good of a time atop the dirt/debris pile, and further that they could have a better time than us by riding their motorcycles on it. The management of the factory forced us to retire from the Shire. I vowed that someday I would restore the spirit of the Shire, a spirit of learning and education.
[Resurrection of Shire]

It has been over a decade now, and our dream of restoring the Shire has been in a state of abeyance. Varner, Conduf, and I have moved to distant states and inevitably our pursuits have taken us down differing paths. How to restore the Shire in this situation? What is needed is a mechanism that can connect distant points as if those points exist in the same or nearby location. The answer is the Internet. What can be done is a creation of a virtual Shire replete with silken scenes and heady thoughts. A virtual Shire existing in the abstract aether of the World Wide Web that asks only one thing of its visitors: come with a mind ready to employ the innate logic that is possessed by every sentient being. Enter the Abstract Shire.
