Physics is what humans have used to procure sustenance: as omnivores, we used physics to calculate (albeit approximately) the trajectory of spears, rocks, and other tools to bring down prey such as birds and bison, and we used the same tools to grow crops and grind meal, etc. Moreover, we cultivate our sustenance today using the same laws of physics that our early ancestors used.

Mastery of the subject granted humankind dominion over the animal kingdom, and guaranteed the survival of the early humans over the Neanderthals. Those species that were able to leverage physics in their day-to-day activities were better able to sustain themselves in hunting / gathering and fighting / defending.

Furthermore, this mastering of the subject will allow humankind (along with other species) to survive the coming eons by continuing the space program to research, visit, perhaps terraform, and colonize other worlds. Specializations such as Nanophysics will produce efficacious vaccines to erradicate the world's most recalcitrant diseases. Nanophysics and Neurophysics, when they have matured, will further guarrantee humankind's destiny by merging with evolution to create the artificially intelligent progeny.

Physics, in its irrefutable mathematical dictions, proves what is possible and impossible.
