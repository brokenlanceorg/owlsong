Introspection

I have heard the term used often by a vast number of people. However, the practitioners of this phrase have mis-interpreted its true meaning.

Introspection is the practice of viewing those ethical, moral, and yes even those simplistic characteristics within yourself. Introspection requires, as much as is possible, an extrinsic view of yourself. Like the existentialist, you must find your inner self by a view which parallels that of an outside stranger. The question you must ask yourself is: “What do I represent and who have I become?”.
