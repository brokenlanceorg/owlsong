This situation is analogous to the problem of observation in Quantum Mechanics wherein once a sentient entity makes an observation to a quantum system, the system is affected in an unpredictable way. Here, as soon as a theory, model, or description of the real existential world is constructed, we have ‘built-in’ to the theory undecidable or emergent-like properties.

Perhaps emergent properties should be redefined as the undecidable propositions, otherwise, science would be well-served by taking a logical-positivist stance and consider questions concerning ‘emergence’ or ‘emergent properties’ meaningless since they can never be verified. Moreover, any attempt to secure a ‘final’ or ‘universally complete’ theory is futile, e.g., modern physics in its attempt to find the ‘theory of everything’ (TOE). One final conclusion can be made: it is now time to stop the philosophical sophistry and get on with science.
