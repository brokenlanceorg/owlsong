Fakeville

The town lies there lifeless in an elitist type of zone
This place no longer welcomes me even though it’s home
During waking hours it seems like all the rest
The people look on frantically at every passing guest.


The citizens have gathered from places far and near
To cover-up their past-times and hide away their fear.
Each one finds enjoyment in knowing no one leaves
Their ignorance and pride yields but fruitless trees


Meanwhile the rest of the world finds new paths by which to travel
As the people in the small town let their lives unravel
Journeyman, pioneer, and adventurer both strait and gay
If they ever enter I guarantee they’ll stay
