[V Derivation of the Activation Function]

We have determined everything necessary for a complete implementation of a backpropagation neural network except for the activation function s(x) referenced throughout section III and IV. First, recognize that the activation function is equivalent with the y’s in equation (I.2). Given this fact, we know that the activation functions, s(x), are monotonically increasing on the interval E, and must be sigmoid (S-shaped) in nature to capture non-linear threshold logic behavior.

Next, the knowns for the problem should be considered:

  • s(x) is the output down the virtual neuron’s axon
  • x is the weighted sum of incoming impulses to the neuron
  • s(x) is monotonically increasing on the interval E
  • s(x) is sigmoid in shape
  • most impulses impinging on a neuron are excitory, not inhibitory.
  • Impulses have been seen to be modulatory in order to control feedback and other pernicious possibilities.
