That Hilbert’s thirteenth problem has more than a curious relevance to connectionist artificial intelligence is not obvious. As with most interesting mathematical problems, the demesne, including both the problem space and the solution space, typically turns out to be more applicable to various disciplines not formally intended. This new-found applicability to other pursuits is the situation for Hilbert’s thirteenth problem, for it is the case that Hilbert’s thirteenth problem may be understood as a special case of the computational structure in artificial intelligence known as the artificial neural network. To be sure, Hilbert was unaware of this connection if not for the simple fact that cybernetics was only in its incipient stages during the turn of the century, but he obviously deemed it of particular significance being that it was included in his famous paper presented to the Second International Congress of Mathematicians.
