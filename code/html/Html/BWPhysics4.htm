[The Language of Physics]

All of physics, hence the rest of science, can be derived from the laws of the universe, which are elegant, consise mathematical axioms. The laws of physics are the result of logical thinking and applied mathematics. As the scope of physics broadens and phenomena renders itself to theory, the mathematical language used to elucidate this theory becomes more abstract. And with abstraction comes beauty.
