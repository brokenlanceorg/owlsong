When performing numerical computations, the slightest error can become magnified to such a degree that the entire calculation’s validity becomes suspect.

Since any computer is finite in scope, simply representing a number such as p or e involves, technically, an infinite amount of error. It is the responsibility of the numerical analyst to ensure that errors don’t infect the calculation beyond a certain error tolerance.
