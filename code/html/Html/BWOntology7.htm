To get a handle on what the future will be like, observe the following adroit observation that has been made by several scientific cognoscenti: technology is progressing at an exponential rate. A quick, but thorough, rumination will show that this statement is quite profound.

Note that any exponential process will appear slower than a linearly growing process in the incipient stages, but as time progresses, the exponential process explodes into infinity from the limited perspective of the linear process. Take a moment to imagine what exponential growth means to an evolutionary process. Each tier in the process is used to manufacture the next, and so on exponentially.

A simple, but illustrative, example of an exponential process is that of the inventor of chess and his patron, the emperor of China. When asked by the emperor what he desired as reward for his invention, the inventor requested that he be given the grains of rice resulting from placing one grain on the first square, two on the second, four on the third, and so on to the end of the chess board. The emperor granted the seemingly feckless request.

Through the first half of the chessboard things seemed fine, by the end of the chessboard (at the 64th square), the emperor was entitled to give the inventor over 18 million trillion grains of rice. At ten grains of rice per square inch, it would take a field over twice the size of the surface area of the Earth, oceans included, to yield such a crop.

Needless to say, an exponential process is nothing to sneeze at.
