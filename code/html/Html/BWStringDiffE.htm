[Derivation of the Wave Function]

In order to determine the wave function, it is necessary to first derive the wave equation. The wave equation, or wave differential equation, will give us a class of functions when solved that will model the behavior of wave phenomena. We begin by considering a one-dimensional (one space dimension together with a time dimension) wave form moving through a medium that has m mass per unit of length and tension T, thus it can be shown that the velocity of the resulting wave is v = Ö ( T / m ). Our goal is to determine the shape of the wave as a function of the x-axis and the time-axis: y(x, t).

Consider two observers whose space coordinates are x and x'. The wave form is maintaining the same shape as it moves through space, thus, the two observers should describe the same shape with respect to their space coordinate so that, at any given time, we should have:

y = ¦(x) = ¦(x')

Now observe that when x' = 0, x = vt because the wave form is moving through space at velocity v. Since x = vt implies that x - vt = 0, x and x' are related accordingly as: x' = x - vt.
