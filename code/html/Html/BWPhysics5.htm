First, the fundamental laws of physics are extremely simple. The equations and principles are easily understood by anyone. The mathematical statements that embody these laws are consise and elegant. Yet, these simple equations engender vast complexities that can explain the universe in its physical form--the equations are only limited by our computational ability.


Second, the equations constrain what the verdical 'reality' must be like. We know from the equations and experiments that the universe is not full of 'ether'.

we know that the Sun does not revolve around the Earth; we know that matter is engergy and that matter exists both as waves and particles. We will never know with infinite precision what the universe is really like, but we can place severe restrictions on what that reality can be like.

Finally, physics shows us that the universe is a wonderous thing. There is a euphoric sense of awe and wonderment to understanding exactly how nature does what it does.
