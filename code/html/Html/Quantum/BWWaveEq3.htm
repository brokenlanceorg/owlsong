Born’s interpretation is the following: the probability of finding a particle in a small volume of space dV located at position r is given by y*(r) y(r) dV, where y*(r) is the complex conjugate of y(r).

This interpretation has been verified experimentally to extremely high levels of certainty. It implies that we really can’t say exactly where a particle is, and in fact, Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle shows this is true.
