[The Michelson-Morely Experiment]

The Michelson-Morely experiment was originally meant to measure the speed of the aether wind. The aether was a hypothetical medium in which light traveled, much in the same way sound travels in air. The experiment proved conclusively that ipso facto there was no aether.

Aside from this result, there were other conclusions that could be drawn. If we observe that light travels at the same velocity regardless of frame of reference, we can conclude that length contraction and time dilation is possible for 'moving' frames of reference, i.e., frames of reference moving with respect to some other frame of reference.

The Michelson-Morely experiment consisted of an apparatus that consisted of a source of light aimed at a half-silvered mirror.

Half of the photons would pass through the mirror and continue a straight path to a mirror where they would reflect back to the starting point, while the other half of the photons would reflect off the half-silvered mirror on an orthogonal path towards another mirror, at which point they would reflect back to the starting point.
