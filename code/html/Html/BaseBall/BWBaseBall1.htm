[The Night of the All-Star Announcements]


Even though the day was blisteringly hot, the night seemed a little chilly with the brisk wind blowing through the ballpark. The stadium lights always make the night feel cooler than it really is by casting their eerie-blue glow over the bleachers, the audience, the chain-link fence, everything.

I loved watching games from the top row of the bleachers─it gave me the euphoric feeling of watching the action from above. I was used to seeing the game at the field level through a player's eyes, but on this particular night, I was only a spectator at the last regular-season game.

I felt sick with anticipation and anxiety, or maybe it was from the smell of the nearby chicken plant─the brisk wind brought more than coolness with it. After the game, the year's All-Star team would be named.

I can almost smell the air and hear the sounds as if I were there again watching with a mixture of emotions. Yes….I can see it now…..It looks like the whole town is packed into Tyson park tonight; there's standing room only. (Actually, I wouldn't know if the stands are this full during regular-season games. When I was actually playing, I made it a point never to look into the stands. My dad had told me that a player's focus should be on the game, otherwise, concentration, along with the game, would be lost.)

"Do you think we'll make it?" asks the pudgy boy.

"I don't know," I reply instinctively. Morgan has been asking Varn and me that question all night long. I wish he would quit asking.

Without saying anything, Varn nudges me on the shoulder and casually points down to the front of the bleachers. Scanning through all the people walking to and fro, I spot McKee strutting along the backstop trailed by three nice-looking girls.
